# Dream Todo List
A list of things I want to add when I have time to work on this more. This can range from far off ideas that sound neat to things that I'm actively working on already.

## Combat
- Ranged weaponry with visual effects (much like the existing turrets). Can you really be a group of wizards setting up a colony if you can't blast a raider with a magic missle? Based on my want for Evocation below I think the best way to do this is by adding ranged magic combat spells that can be used by a colonist regardless of what weapon they have equipped at the time. Then we can add specialized equipment that boosts magic spell damage (i.e. wands, staves) but don't work as well in melee combat so wizards have options to use once they know a sufficient number of combat spells.

## Colonists
- Add skill(s) for learning magic abilities. Colonists can have their own specializations and focus on different types of spells to help the colony. This might include effects similar to existing potions, like healing or making work go faster, or simple combat skills with wands and staves (perhaps the only way we should do ranged combat?).

The magic system could include:
1. Evocation - Ranged combat spells
- Magic Missle
2. Enchantment - Support spells for everyday tasks (and golems!)
- Haste, Animate Golem
3. Abjuration - Combat-focused support spells
- Heal, Shield (% damage reduction, like armor)
4. Conjuration - Summoning and teleporting
- Summon Familiar, Warp
5. Transmutation - Spells that help re-shape the map environment (think creating lakes, deserts, forests, mountains, etc.), speed up animal product production, and increase crop production
- Circle of Growth, Raise Mountain, Level Field (creates a grassy field with nothing in it, destroying whatever terrain was there previously and replacing it with grass)

Perhaps colonists can learn spells by studying spell tomes that can either be crafted or obtained through traders. If a colonist has a tome equipped and goes to study for research points, they will also make progress in the tome (showing a percentage of completion for that colonist in their info screen). This will increase the respective magic skill level like performing any other skill would. Once they complete a tome it will consume it and the colonist will now have access to that spell on their spell list, which they will cast automatically when needed. It may also be nice to have control over a colonist's spell casting for things like re-shaping the map or forcing them to teleport to specific places. Most spells should work automatically, much like potions, and need no oversight by the player.

Colonists can have a "mana" stat that increases based on their current combined magic skill levels (to encourage training multiple magic schools for each colonist). When casting a spell, it will consume some amount of mana and be put on a cooldown so they cannot cast it again too quickly.

## Magic
- Complex structures for complex crafting. I like the idea of late-game craftables requiring a specific structure built around some crafting furniture, much like the multi-block machines folks made in Minecraft mods back in the day. This could be paired with the guaranteed generation of a ruin for this structure on the map that you can later repair.
- I adore golems and would love to make a system where you can create golems for different tasks. Basically a colonist who specializes in one job and never sleeps. A little farmer golem or a strong combat golem with some customization to help them do their given task better.

## Animals
- Taming system could include all types of beasts, including wolves. I love the Rimworld pet system and I like the idea of animals being used for more than just animal product production.

## Trade
- More complex trading system where you can barter any of your items for the goods you need. Right now this system boils down to "Exchange X of A for Y of B" and doesn't really add a ton to the game.
- Exclusive items that can be traded for, like artifacts, tools, armor, or weapons.
